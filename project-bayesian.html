Bayesian Data Assimilation on Arctic Glaciers

Learning from Large Ensemble Twin Experiments

Overview

This research develops and applies Bayesian data assimilation methods to improve predictions of Arctic glacier mass balance. By combining observational data with physical models through ensemble-based techniques, we can better understand glacier dynamics and quantify uncertainty in our predictions.

Methodology

The project employs Observing System Simulation Experiments (OSSE) to evaluate data assimilation strategies before applying them to real observations. Key methodological components include:

  • Ensemble Kalman Filter: For optimal state estimation by combining model predictions with observations
  • Twin Experiments: Using synthetic observations generated from a known "truth" to validate assimilation schemes
  • Uncertainty Quantification: Rigorous estimation of prediction uncertainties through ensemble spread analysis
  • Large Ensemble Approach: Leveraging computational resources for robust statistical inference

Application Domain

The methods are applied to glacier systems in Svalbard, a critical region for understanding Arctic climate change. The research integrates:

  • Satellite remote sensing observations
  • In-situ field measurements from expeditions
  • Surface mass balance models
  • Glacier dynamics simulations

Key Contributions

  • Framework for evaluating data assimilation strategies in glaciological applications
  • Insights into optimal observation strategies for glacier monitoring
  • Improved understanding of uncertainty propagation in glacier predictions
  • Transferable methodology applicable to other environmental prediction systems
