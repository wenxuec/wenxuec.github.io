Background

I'm a Marie-Curie funded researcher working on the CompSci Project at the Department of Geosciences, University of Oslo. My work focuses on observing system simulation experiments (OSSE) through glacier observational data assimilation in Svalbard.

With a background spanning Geographic Information Systems (Sun Yat-Sen University) and Global Change Geography (Humboldt University of Berlin), I combine computational expertise with deep domain knowledge in climate science.

Ph.D. Geosciences University of Oslo (2021-Present)

Research Methodology

My research centers on Bayesian Data Assimilation - a powerful framework for combining observational data with physical models to improve predictions and quantify uncertainty.

B

Bayesian Data Assimilation

Developing ensemble-based data assimilation methods that integrate satellite observations, in-situ measurements, and glacier dynamics models. Using Bayesian inference to optimally combine prior knowledge with new observations while rigorously quantifying uncertainty.

Ensemble Kalman Filter Particle Filter Uncertainty Quantification
G

Glacier Mass Balance Modeling

Applying data assimilation to improve glacier mass balance estimates in Svalbard. Combining remote sensing data with surface mass balance models to better understand Arctic glacier response to climate change.

OSSE Mass Balance Svalbard
M

Machine Learning & Statistics

Integrating machine learning approaches with traditional Bayesian methods for improved parameter estimation and model calibration in complex glaciological systems.

Statistical Modeling ML Integration Parameter Estimation
