Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage with ESG

Strategic Integration within Global ESG Frameworks

Overview

This research examines the strategic integration of Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS) technologies within global Environmental, Social, and Governance (ESG) frameworks. The project bridges climate technology with sustainable finance and corporate responsibility.

Research Focus

The study addresses critical questions at the intersection of climate technology and sustainable business practices:

  • ESG Integration: How CCUS projects align with and enhance corporate ESG performance metrics
  • Policy Frameworks: Analysis of regulatory environments supporting CCUS deployment
  • Investment Strategies: Financial mechanisms for scaling carbon capture technologies
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Balancing environmental goals with social and governance considerations

Key Themes

  • Carbon capture technologies and their role in decarbonization pathways
  • ESG reporting standards and CCUS project evaluation
  • Global policy landscape for carbon management
  • Sustainable investment frameworks for climate technologies

Relevance

This research demonstrates the application of analytical and strategic thinking to global sustainability challenges, bridging technical climate solutions with business and policy considerations. It reflects a broader interest in how quantitative methods can inform decision-making in environmental sustainability.
